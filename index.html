AlgoBridge — Community Impact

Graduation Pitch · Grade 11 · MNB Modern Senior Secondary School
AlgoBridge
Algorithm + Bridge — a bridge that connects algorithms to everyday life.

Millions of students graduate without ever understanding algorithms or AI — the engines powering the modern world. Witnessing how DOT Glasses transformed lives with simple clear vision, I saw a parallel: digital literacy is the clear vision of the 21st century. So I built AlgoBridge — running 4 interactive workshops for students aged 11–18 in underserved schools, with zero prior coding knowledge required. The results exceeded every expectation.

SESSION 01
Algorithms Basics & Decision Making
Introduced algorithms as step-by-step decision systems — no jargon, no code. Just logic expressed through everyday choices. Students became the algorithm themselves.
Decision Tree Slides Predict the Flow Human Algorithm
SESSION 02
How Google Search Works
Brought AI to life through one of the most familiar tools: Google Search. By reverse-engineering how results are ranked, students became the search algorithm.
Page Ranking Game Link Counting Bias Discussion
SESSION 03
What is Coding & Debugging?
Demystified coding as simply giving instructions to a computer. Interactive debugging exercises — students clicked bugs to fix code — made abstract concepts click instantly.
Robot Instructions Bug Fixing Activity Sandwich Algorithm
SESSION 04
AI in Search & Real-World Impact
Showed how AI reads context, not just keywords — using Mercury as an example (Planet? Element? Band?). Students ran live search experiments and compared results.
Live Search Experiment AI Context Demo Discussion Round
Outcomes & Impact
✅ High engagement and active participation from all students
✅ Students asked deep, curious questions about AI and tech
✅ Improved foundational understanding of algorithms and AI
✅ Even beginners grasped key concepts clearly
What I Took Away
Interactivity is Everything
The moment students had to make a choice, attention sharpened instantly.
Simplicity Bridges Complexity
Once a student saw an algorithm in a recipe, everything clicked.
Empathy Drives Better Teaching
Understanding what students didn't know was more valuable than knowing the subject.

"If a student walks away thinking 'I get it' — that is the whole point."
