The Problem

Millions of students graduate without ever encountering algorithms or AI — the engines powering the modern world. 1 in 3 students lack basic digital literacy. In underserved schools, the number is far worse. Not because students lack ability — but because they lack access. No trained teachers. No infrastructure. No awareness of why it matters.

AlgoBridge
Bridging the gap between algorithms and every student's future.

I designed and ran a workshop-based AI literacy program for students aged 11–18, with zero prior coding knowledge required. No jargon, no textbooks — just real-world examples, interactive decisions, and the belief that understanding AI should not be a privilege. Inspired by witnessing how DOT Glasses transformed lives with simple clear vision, I saw the parallel: one workshop on algorithms could spark a future engineer.

Students actively explained algorithms using real-world analogies — unprompted
High engagement sustained across all 4 sessions vs near-zero in traditional lectures
Beginners grasped concepts like bias, ranking, and decision-making within one session
Students asked deep questions about AI that went beyond the curriculum
